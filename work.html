Comparing Neural Representations: Theoretical vs. Deep Learning Models
active NeurIPS 2025 VSS 2026 talk
Independent Research· July 2025 – Present
PyTorchNumPy fMRIRSAHMAX
  • analyzed neural similarity between human fMRI data and computational models, comparing classical mathematical models (HMAX, Gabor pyramids) to state-of-the-art deep networks
  • key finding: HMAX exhibits stronger alignment with early visual cortex (V1) activity compared to recent hyper-complex architectures
  • developed a cross-model representational similarity framework to quantify correspondence between model activations and voxel-level brain patterns
  • conducted layer-wise and feature-space comparisons using PyTorch to probe hierarchical encoding across architectures
  • accepted as extended abstract at UniReps, NeurIPS 2025
  • accepted as contributed talk at VSS 2026 (Vision Sciences Society Annual Meeting, May 2026)
CNN Analysis of Background Distractors in Visual Search
active URCA Grant
Vision & Image Understanding Lab, UCSB· Feb 2025 – Present
PyTorchNumPy scikit-learnPython

Computational Model of Dynamic Gaze Cueing
may 2024
Computational Modeling· May 2024
PythonTensorFlow Drift Diffusion ModelingBayesian
  • implemented three frameworks: a Bayesian Ideal Observer, a 3D CNN for emergent anticipatory orienting, and a time-resolved Drift Diffusion Model with contrast-response functions
  • calibrated DDM parameters using 700 Hz synthetic luminance inputs to replicate human-like anticipatory integration and faster RT on valid gaze cues
  • validated against human psychophysics (9 observers, 10,000 trials) via reverse-correlation logistic regression — demonstrated precise replication of key gaze-cueing effects
